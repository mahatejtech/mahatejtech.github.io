About Maha Tej Technologies

Mission

To engineer sustainable, efficient, and intelligent solutions that harmonize advanced computational technologies with the principles of nature. Maha Tej Technologies integrates CFD, renewable energy innovation and green building design to reduce energy loss, optimise performance and promote a cleaner planet through research, precision and ethical engineering practice.

Vision

To become a global benchmark in sustainable computational engineering, empowering industries to transition towards a carbon-neutral future. Guided by the wisdom of ancient Indian science and the discipline of modern technology, Maha Tej Technologies envisions a world where energy flows as purely as thought — efficient, regenerative and seemingly infinite.

Fields of Operation

Renewable Energy Systems (Hydro, Wind, Solar)

Design and simulation of energy systems using CFD and thermodynamic modelling to maximise energy extraction and efficiency. Focus areas include hydrokinetic turbine optimisation, solar heat-transfer enhancement and wind-energy aerodynamics.

Automotive Efficiency & Sustainable Transport

Advanced flow, cooling and aerodynamics simulations for low-emission vehicles and hydrogen propulsion systems. CFD-driven innovation to reduce drag, improve combustion and optimise thermal management for a greener automotive industry.

HVAC & Green Building Systems

Comprehensive CFD analysis of ventilation, thermal comfort and air quality for smart and sustainable building environments. Solutions aligned with BREEAM, LEED and Net Zero objectives to enable eco-efficient architectural design.

Selected Projects

CFD · R&D

Hydrokinetic Turbine Flow Analysis

Horizontal-axis turbine in canal-like conditions with performance comparison across operating points.

Set up and ran CFD simulations to understand velocity distribution, torque behaviour and loading on blades under varying flow rates. Used the results to compare configurations and identify operating regions with smoother torque and improved predicted efficiency.

Tools: OpenFOAM, CAD for geometry prep, ParaView for post-processing.

HVAC · Pressure Drop

Duct Network Pressure Loss Study

Concept-level optimisation of an HVAC duct layout to reduce fan power.

Analysed a branching duct network, estimating pressure losses and identifying problematic bends and junctions. Recommended layout changes and sizing adjustments aimed at reducing system resistance and improving distribution.

Tools: CAD, analytical calculations, basic CFD checks, spreadsheets.

Energy Systems · Simulation

Electric Vehicle Charging Impact Study

Simulation-based assessment of EV charging schedules on a distribution network.

Evaluated how different charging patterns influence network loading and losses, using optimisation-style approaches to shift demand and reduce stress on the system. Summarised outcomes as clear charts and recommendations.

Tools: Numerical scripting, optimisation routines, data visualisation.

Thermal · Concept Design

Cooling Flow Concept for High-Heat Components

Channel and plenum concept study for more uniform cooling.

Developed concept geometries for coolant flow around heat-generating components, then used simplified simulations and calculations to compare designs. Focused on temperature uniformity and pressure drop trade-offs.

Tools: CAD, CFD, post-processing and comparative plots.
